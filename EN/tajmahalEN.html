Taj Mahal

Indian mausoleum erected by Shah Jahan of the Mughal dynasty in memory of the prematurely deceased beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. The object is sometimes called the temple of love. The Taj Mahal was erected in Agra, a city on the Jamuna River in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It consists of the main mausoleum with a large onion-shaped dome, characteristic of Islamic art, and a huge gate, symbolizing the gate to Paradise. It is set on a plinth with four minarets in the corners - towers from which the muezzin calls the faithful to prayer. On the sides are two identical redstone mosques. The complex is complemented by standard elements of this type of structure: water canals and a landscaped garden divided into four parts.
