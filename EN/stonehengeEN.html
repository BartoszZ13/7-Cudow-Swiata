Stonehenge

One of the most famous European megalithic structures, dating from the Neolithic and Bronze Age. This lumberjack is situated 8 miles from Salisbury Town in Wiltshire in southern England. Most likely he was associated with the cult of the Moon and the Sun. The moon could symbolize a woman here, the Sun - a man. It consists of earth dikes surrounding a large group of standing stones. The property has been inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List since 1986. Stonehenge consists of successive elements, set at long intervals (the sanctuary was built gradually, over a thousand years). The avenue leading to Stonehenge is about 3 km long and 11 m wide. Originally its edges were limited by an earth embankment.
