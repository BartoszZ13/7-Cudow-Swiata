Colosseo

An amphitheater in Rome, built in the years 70-72 to 80 CE by Vespasian and Titus - emperors of the Flavian dynasty. It is a large, elliptical building with a spacious audience that could accommodate 45,000 to 50,000 spectators; with communication galleries and an arena with a system of underground corridors. The three lower storeys are connected with the structural arrangement of the arcades, the fourth, highest storey has only small windows. There were, among others, gladiators fights, naumachie, hunting wild animals. Tradition says that Christians were murdered in Colosseo, which was commemorated with a cross inside the building. Colosseo has been under protection as the place of martyrdom of the first Christians since the middle of the 18th century.
