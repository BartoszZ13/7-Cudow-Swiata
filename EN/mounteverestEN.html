Mount Everest

The highest peak of the Earth (8,848 m above sea level), eight-thousanders located in the High Himalayas, on the border of Nepal and the People's Republic of China. On the Chinese side, it is protected as part of the biosphere reserve called the Chomolungma Biosphere Reserve. It is built of granite, gneiss, limestone and slate. It creates a huge massif undercut on three sides by glaciers (the longest Khumbu and Rongbuk - approx. 17 km). The height of the peak in relation to the foot of the mountain is approximately 3700 meters. The walls of Mount Everest are about 3 kilometers high. It was considered the seat of the gods by the local population. First measured by British surveyors in the mid-nineteenth century, first captured on May 29, 1953.
