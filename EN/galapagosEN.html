Galapagos

Galapagos, also known as the Turtle Islands, is an archipelago of volcanic origin in the Pacific Ocean, at the equator, about 1000 km west of the coast of South America. The islands belong to Ecuador, and their area is approx. 8 thousand. km². In 1978, the archipelago was included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. The archipelago consists of 13 islands with an area between 14 and 4588 km², 6 smaller ones and over 100 islets and rocks with a total area of 8010 km² scattered over an area of 59.5 thousand. km². The islands are famous for their numerous endemics - over 80% of all land birds, 97% of reptiles and land mammals, over 20% of marine species, and 30% of all plants found on the islands are endemic species.
